\chapter{Introduction} \label{cha:introduction}
\pagestyle{plain}

The \textbf{Introduction} sets the stage for your thesis. It introduces the research problem and poses the research question(s). In other words, it addresses both the overarching question: \textit{Why is this study important?} and the specific aim: \textit{What is this study to achieve?} 

The introduction should also provide enough background to make your work comprehensible to your intended audience, even if they are not specialists in your specific subdomain. A good rule of thumb is to keep the introduction concise—usually no more than 1 (or maximum 1.5) page.

\subsection*{Key Components to Include}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Context and Motivation:} Briefly describe the broader context and explain why the topic is relevant. Situate the work within the field of AI and Language and, if applicable, its societal or technological relevance.
  
  \item \textbf{Research Problem:} Clearly state the problem you aim to address, without going into technical or methodological details.

  \item \textbf{Brief Literature Context:} Offer a concise and neutral summary of the current research landscape. This is not a full literature review—that will come later—but just enough to establish what has already been done and where your work fits.
    
  \item \textbf{Purpose and Scope:} Clarify the overall goal of your thesis and set appropriate expectations for the reader. Using precise language. Suitable verbs include:
  \textit{investigate, explore, evaluate, analyze, compare, describe, map}. Avoid vague or overly general aims. The purpose should reflect both the research gap and your specific contribution.
  
  \item \textbf{Research Questions:} Present the main research question(s) your thesis investigates. These questions guide the rest of the thesis, and you should be able to answer them in the end.

 \item \textbf{Collaboration:} If the thesis is conducted as part of a larger research project, or in collaboration with a company, university, or research institute, briefly describe the nature of the collaboration and its relevance to the thesis. Mention the names of the collaborating institutions, and summarize the project’s broader goals if applicable.

  \item \textbf{Disposition:} Provide a brief overview of the structure of the thesis. Describe what each chapter covers to help the reader navigate the document.

\end{enumerate}
