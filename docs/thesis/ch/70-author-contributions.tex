\chapter{Author Contributions} \label{cha:contributions}

When writing your thesis together, you should include a short statement describing each author’s contributions. This helps clarify the division of work while acknowledging joint efforts. The statement does not need to be long, but it should be specific. 

For example:
Indicate which parts were primarily written or developed by each student (e.g., literature review, data collection, analysis, or writing of certain chapters).
Mention tasks that were carried out jointly (e.g., planning, discussion of results, editing).
Ensure that the contributions are balanced and transparent, and each participated actively in all stages of the thesis. 
\bigskip

\textit{Example format}:
"Student A was primarily responsible for the literature review and data analysis. Student B was primarily responsible for the data collection and discussion chapter. Both authors jointly planned the study, formulated the research questions, and revised the final manuscript."
